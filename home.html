Personas & Storyboard

The Dishwasher

Description & Sketch

The goal of the dishwasher is to wash, clean, and dry kitchenware, yet it seems that many users struggle when interfacing with the device. With many different settings and cryptic manuals, users must endure several rounds of trial and error to get the dishwasher performing the way they would like. Once configured, the lack of feedback results in accidentally opening the dishwasher before it is completed, having little idea about when their dishes are dry, and many other issues that could be resolved with a more intuitive and informative interface.

Sketch

User Data

Interview Questions

  1. What is the purpose of the dishwasher? Does it achieve this?
  2. What does each button on the interface do?
  3. What steps do you take to run the device?
  4. How did you learn to operate the device?
  5. When have you been frustrated with the dishwasher?
  6. When have you been satisfied with the dishwasher?
  7. Is running the dishwasher a task you look forward
  8. Are there any missing features you wish this device had?

Interview Responses

Similarities Between Users

All the subjects agreed that how the buttons correspond to how the machine washes the dishes is very unclear. Specifically with the two Option buttons (left and right), subjects did not know the difference between Heat Dry, High Temp, and Heat Dry and High Temp. None of the subjects interact with Option Right button, but all of them agreed that it is likely important to the operation of the machine. The two light indicators on the side of the device were a mystery to all the subjects; they were unclear about what Clean and Sanitize indicators are referring to. Interestingly, all the subjects, upon pushing the start button, put their ear to the machine and listened for if it was working. They reported having no indication about if their sequence of button presses resulted in the machine starting. Finally, they all agree that while knowing the dishwasher is currently running takes a burden off their chores, they fear the moment of opening up the machine and seeing dirty dishes.

Differences Between Users

From the interviews conducted, the primary differentiators between subjects was their understanding of the buttons, overall satisfaction of the machine, and operating procedure. From the three people interviewed, each explained the Cycle button differently: corresponds to the number of dishes in the dishwasher, pressure and method of spraying water, duration of the wash. Surprisingly, even the Start button had different protocols for each user: press and hold, press twice in quick succession, press once then waited 3 seconds and press again. Once the machine was running, subjects reportly accidentally opening the dishwasher because they did not know it was still running. Some subjects believed they were operating the machine correctly, while others believed that with a different series of button presses they could get a better outcome.


General Observations

  • Some of the subjects presented ideas for adding features to the dishwasher including:
    • adding a timer to see when the wash would be completed
    • adding a more clear indicator about when the dishwahser was running. Potentially a locking device that would prevent the user from opening the door while it was running.
    • adding a window so the user could see the washing in action, causing more satisfaction
  • The learning process was an important part of the overall experience for the users
    • some were shown by the previous user of the washing machine, while others learned through trial and error.
    • none of the subjects reported consulting a manual or getting certain defintions for how the interface affects how the dishwahser cleans.
    • clearly, learning to use the dishwasher was not effective given all the subjects doubt their ability to fully control the operations of the machine to produce desired outcomes
  • What users are looking for in their dishwasher
    • while subjects noted that it is, at times, enjoyable to wash dishes, having the assistance of a dishwahser, especially during busy times of the week, relieves stress and free up time
    • the job of the dishwasher is the same for all subjects: wash, clean, and dry kitchenware
    • lastly, subjects all noted that if the dishwasher is doing its job, then they should not have to think too much about it; unfortunately, the interface on this dishwasher leads to constant problems

Personas

Persona #1

Bob is a college student who is often stressed with school work. He is generally disorganized and needs to pay full attention to a given task in order to complete it. Given that Bob is living off-campus for the first time, he has had several added responsibilities - the most time consuming for him being cleaning the dishes. While loading the dishwasher is not too difficult, he never truly learned the ideal settings to run the machine with; he is usually in a rush and ends up just spamming start, regardless of the current active settings. He had used washing machine before that simply worked without him playing with the settings and believed this one would do the same. Unfortunately for Bob, he consistently opens the dishwasher to find dirty dishes. Bob has to run his dishwasher numerous times, all the while piling up dirty dishes in sink and driving up his utility bill.

Bob represents the users because he is not open to learning exactly how to use the device, is often in a rush when interfacing with the dishwasher, and expects the device to work without much of their own effort. Not all users of dishwashers can invest the time to read a manual or run through many iterations of settings to find the ideal one. Bob, like many of the users, see the dishwasher as a simple device that should simply work. When it does, the Bob and users get frustrated.

Persona #2

Steve is a father, husband, and in his mid-50s. He enjoys spending his weekends working on projects around the house and spending time with his family. He also is the designated cleanup crew of the family. After dinner, he pops in his AirPods and zones out to a podcast while scrubbing the dishes. He loads the dishes into the dishwasher and has a refined procedure of settings he follows every time. He knows exactly which buttons are needed and could explain how each functions. When he first purchased the dishwasher, he skimmed the manual and tried out different configurations himself until he found one that worked best. He feels good knowing that he found the best settings to clean his dishes well.

Steve represents the users who have time and patience to seek out how to best use the dishwasher. The idea of not understanding how the machine works and how the buttons cause changing in its performance nags at these users. They were initially frustrated that the machine required them to take time to learn rather than being intuitive, but are now satisfied with its operation.

Storyboard

After a long day of classes, Bob returns stresses knowing he has a midterm tomorrow.

He cooks himself a nice steak dinner.

He loads his dirty dishes into the dishwahser...

and, without changing any settings, pushes the start button

Bob gets right to work studying for his midterm exam.

The next morning, Bob is feeling well-rested and ready for his exam. All he needs is a good breakfast to ace the midterm!

Bob goes to grab a bowl from the dishwasher, but realizes the dishes are still dirty. He is frustrated, having no idea why the machine didn't do what he expected.

Out of time, Bob storms out of his home with an empty stomach, feeling much worse about his exam...and his dishwasher.
